An overview of REfigure:

Typically, when opened by clicking on the REfigure icon in your browser, the sidebar will look like this:

  1. The top section of the sidebar will feature REfigures that include images that have been linked by you, or other users on the current article page. Such REfigures are indicated on the active REfigure icon, for example displaying 1/1 for 1 image on this page located in 1 REfigure.
  2. The search bar allows you to search for a REfigure you are interested in, for example, if you wanted to add a figure to a current REfigure (currently you can only edit REfigures that you have created)
  3. The lower section features the most recent REfigures that you have created

Creating an account at REfigure.org

  1. Navigate to refigure.org
  2. Click on the user icon in the top right of the page to sign in/sign up
  3. Click on "Sign Up" at the bottom of the main page
  4. Follow the on screen prompts to setup your account
  5. An email will be auto-generated and sent to your provided email account to confirm signup

Once your account is active, you can use your login and password to sign into REfigure.org and the browser extension when prompted. Follow the next steps to install the web extension:

Downloading and installing REfigure from the Chrome Store:

  1. Navigate to chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/refigure/plbplbglohkmicpcelhkendkdfaipome at the Chrome Store
  2. Click on "Add to Chrome" and allow the permissions requested at the prompt to install the extension to your web browser
You are now ready to use REfigure! Here are some step-by-step instructions to creating your first REfigure:

Creating a REfigure:

You can see a video demo for creating REfigures on our YouTube Channel here

  1. Navigate to the article you are interested in on one of the following 4 domains:
    • ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/
    • plos.org
    • elifesciences.org
    • figshare.com
  2. Click on the REfigure icon (usually found to the right of your address bar)
  3. When the sidebar opens, select "Create New Refigure" at the bottom of the sidebar
  4. Fill in a title, description and keywords for your new REfigure
  5. Click on "Create" to start adding figures
  6. All of the figures on the article page you are viewing will now be highlighted in a green border; click on the figures you want to add to your REfigure
  7. After clicking on the images, you will see the image its associated metadata are captured in the REfigure sidebar
  8. You may edit the meta-data fields that are captured as you see fit
  9. Once you are done adding figures from the active page, click on "Close" at the bottom of the sidebar to close

Adding figures to your existing REfigure:

  1. Navigate to the article you are interested in on one of the following 4 domains:
    • ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/
    • plos.org
    • elifesciences.org
    • figshare.com
  2. Click on the REfigure icon (usually found to the right of your address bar)
  3. When the sidebar opens, click on the REfigure that you want to add to from the list located below the search bar (your most recent REfigures will be displayed below the search bar, for older REfigures use the search bar)
  4. After selecting the REfigure, the current figures in that REfigure will be displayed in the sidebar, and all of the figures on the current page will be highlighted in a green border
  5. Click on the figures you want to add to your current REfigure (this is the same as step 6 under Creating a REfigure)
  6. After clicking on the images, you will see the image its associated metadata are captured in the REfigure sidebar
  7. You may edit the meta-data fields that are captured as you see fit
  8. Once you are done adding figures from the active page, click on "Close" at the bottom of the sidebar to close

Adding your own data to a REfigure:

  1. First, navigate to figshare.com and upload your figure as a single figure publication
  2. Navigate to the figshare page that includes your new figure
  3. Follow the steps above for "Adding figures to your existing REfigure" to include your data in an existing REfigure, or "Creating a REfigure" to start a REfigure with your own data

Exploring ReFigures made by other people:

You can search by author name. The author name is also clickable in each refigure, allowing you to find other refigures by the author of the refigure that you are viewing.
